# Target Users

## Primary Audience

### Public and Private Schools
- **Primary Schools** (Elementary/Primary education, ages 5-11)
- **Secondary Schools** (Junior and Senior Secondary, ages 12-18)
- **Both public (government-funded) and private (tuition-based) institutions**

## User Roles

### 1. School Administrators
**Who they are:**
- Principals, Vice Principals, Academic Directors
- Registrars, Academic Coordinators

**Their needs:**
- Oversight of entire result management process
- Ability to configure grading policies and scales
- Generate school-wide reports and analytics
- Override capabilities for error correction
- Audit trail and accountability tracking

**Their pain points:**
- Lack of centralized control over result processing
- Difficulty ensuring consistency across departments
- Time-consuming manual verification processes
- Limited visibility into overall academic performance trends

---

### 2. Teachers
**Who they are:**
- Subject teachers across all grade levels
- Class teachers/Form tutors
- Department heads

**Their needs:**
- Quick and easy grade entry for their subjects
- Automatic calculation of totals and averages
- Clear view of class performance
- Ability to review and edit entries before final submission
- Minimal technical complexity

**Their pain points:**
- Repetitive manual calculations
- Risk of calculation errors affecting student outcomes
- Time spent on administrative tasks instead of teaching
- Difficulty tracking individual student progress over time

---

### 3. Students
**Who they are:**
- Current students across all grade levels
- Alumni seeking historical records

**Their needs:**
- Easy access to current and past results
- Clear, professional report cards
- Ability to download/print results
- Transparency in grading and ranking

**Their pain points:**
- Delayed access to results
- Lost or damaged physical report cards
- Difficulty comparing performance across terms
- Lack of visibility into how grades are calculated

---

### 4. Parents/Guardians
**Who they are:**
- Parents and legal guardians of students
- Family members involved in student education

**Their needs:**
- Timely access to their children's academic performance
- Historical view of academic progress
- Easy-to-understand report format
- Ability to identify areas where support is needed

**Their pain points:**
- Limited visibility into academic performance
- Delayed communication of results
- Difficulty tracking long-term trends
- No convenient access to historical records

---

## User Expectations

### Common Across All Users
- **Reliability:** System must be available and accurate, especially during critical periods
- **Ease of use:** Intuitive interface requiring minimal training
- **Accessibility:** Responsive design working on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices
- **Security:** Protection of sensitive academic data
- **Speed:** Fast performance even during peak usage periods

### Specific Expectations by Role
- **Administrators:** Control, customization, and comprehensive reporting
- **Teachers:** Simplicity, efficiency, and error prevention
- **Students/Parents:** Transparency, accessibility, and professional presentation
